Health & Body

Suc khoe va co the — mo ta trieu chung, di kham bac si, mua thuoc

Mau cau co ban

Mo ta trieu chung

I have a [symptom]. — Toi bi [trieu chung].
I've been feeling [adj] for [time]. — Toi cam thay [adj] duoc [thoi gian].
It hurts here. — Dau o day. (chi vao cho dau)
I feel dizzy / nauseous / weak. — Toi thay chong mat / buon non / yeu.
My [body part] hurts. — [Bo phan] cua toi bi dau.

Tai phong kham

  • I'd like to make an appointment. — Toi muon dat lich hen.
  • I need to see a doctor. — Toi can gap bac si.
  • How long have you had this problem? — Ban bi nhu vay bao lau roi?
  • Are you taking any medication? — Ban dang uong thuoc gi khong?
  • Are you allergic to anything? — Ban co di ung gi khong?
  • I have health insurance. — Toi co bao hiem y te.

Tai nha thuoc

  • Do you have anything for a headache? — Co thuoc gi tri dau dau khong?
  • How often should I take this? — Uong thuoc nay bao lau mot lan?
  • Take two tablets three times a day. — Uong 2 vien, 3 lan mot ngay.
  • Do I need a prescription for this? — Thuoc nay can don khong?

So sanh voi tieng Viet

Giong: Ca hai ngon ngu deu mo ta trieu chung theo kieu "Toi bi/co [trieu chung]".

Khac:

  • Tieng Anh dung "I have a headache" (co mao tu "a"). Tieng Viet: "Toi bi dau dau" (khong co mao tu).
  • Tieng Anh phan biet "ache" (dau am i) va "pain" (dau nhoi/manh). Tieng Viet chi co "dau".
  • Tieng Anh dung Present Perfect de mo ta trieu chung keo dai: "I've been coughing for 3 days." Tieng Viet: "Toi ho 3 ngay roi."
  • Tieng Anh noi "My head hurts" hoac "I have a headache" — 2 cach. Tieng Viet thuong chi co 1: "Toi bi dau dau".

Dialogue mau

Tinh huong 1: Di kham bac si

Doctor: Good morning. What brings you in today?

You: I've been having a bad cough and sore throat for about four days now.

Doctor: Do you have a fever?

You: Yes, I had a temperature of 38.5 last night.

Doctor: Any other symptoms? Headache, body aches?

You: A bit of a headache, and I feel really tired.

Doctor: Let me take a look at your throat... It's quite red. I think you have a throat infection. I'll prescribe some antibiotics.

You: How long should I take them?

Doctor: Take one tablet twice a day for seven days. Make sure to finish the whole course, even if you feel better.

You: Okay. Should I rest at home?

Doctor: Yes, take a few days off. Drink plenty of fluids and get lots of rest.

Tinh huong 2: Tai nha thuoc

You: Hi, do you have anything for a headache?

Pharmacist: Sure. Do you prefer ibuprofen or paracetamol?

You: What's the difference?

Pharmacist: Ibuprofen is also anti-inflammatory, so it's better if there's any swelling. Paracetamol is gentler on the stomach.

You: I'll take the paracetamol. I have a sensitive stomach.

Pharmacist: Good choice. Take two tablets every four to six hours. Don't exceed eight in 24 hours.

You: Got it. Do I need a prescription?

Pharmacist: No, this is over-the-counter. That'll be $5.50.

Loi thuong gap cua nguoi Viet

  • Sai: "I am headache." → Dung: "I have a headache." (dung "have" voi trieu chung, khong dung "am")
  • Sai: "My head is pain." → Dung: "My head hurts." hoac "I have a headache."
  • Sai: "I sick." → Dung: "I'm sick." hoac "I feel sick." (can dong tu "am" hoac "feel")
  • Sai: "I go to hospital." (khi chi bi cam) → Dung: "I went to the doctor / the clinic." (hospital = benh vien, chi cho truong hop nang)
  • Sai: "I eat medicine." → Dung: "I take medicine." (dung "take" voi thuoc, khong dung "eat")
  • Sai: "I have sick 3 days." → Dung: "I've been sick for 3 days." (Present Perfect + for)

Meo giao tiep

  • "What brings you in today?" — cau hoi bac si thuong dung de bat dau. Tra loi bang trieu chung chinh.
  • Dung Present Perfect khi mo ta trieu chung keo dai: "I've been coughing for 3 days" (khong dung Past Simple).
  • Doctor vs Hospital: O nuoc ngoai, benh nhe di "GP/clinic" (phong kham). "Hospital" chi cho cap cuu hoac benh nang.
  • Over-the-counter (OTC) = thuoc mua khong can don. Prescription = can don bac si.
  • Nho mang the bao hiem — "Do you accept [insurance name]?" khi dat lich.

Bai tap

1. Sua loi: "I am headache since yesterday."

I've had a headache since yesterday.

2. Dien vao cho trong: "I've been ___ (cough) for a week."

coughing (Present Perfect Continuous: have been + V-ing)

3. Sua loi: "I eat medicine every morning."

I take medicine every morning.

4. Ban bi dau hong va sot. Mo ta cho bac si (2-3 cau).

I have a sore throat and a fever. I've been feeling this way for about two days. It hurts when I swallow.

5. Phan biet "ache" va "pain":
a) I have a dull ___ in my back. (dau am i)
b) I felt a sharp ___ in my chest. (dau nhoi)

a) ache (dau am i, keo dai)
b) pain (dau nhoi, manh)

6. Ban muon mua thuoc giam dau o nha thuoc. Hoi the nao?

Do you have anything for a headache? / Can I get some painkillers?

7. Sua loi: "I go to hospital because I have cold."

I went to the doctor/clinic because I have a cold. (hospital = benh vien, chi cho truong hop nang; can mao tu "a" truoc "cold")

8. Duoc dan: "Take two tablets three times a day." — Nghia la uong bao nhieu vien mot ngay?

6 vien/ngay (2 vien x 3 lan = 6)

9. Dien vao cho trong: "Are you ___ to any medication?" (di ung)

allergic (Are you allergic to any medication?)

10. Viet hoi thoai ngan (4-6 cau): ban goi dien dat lich kham bac si.

Vi du:
Receptionist: Good morning, City Clinic. How can I help you?
You: Hi, I'd like to make an appointment with Dr. Smith.
Receptionist: What's the reason for your visit?
You: I've had a bad cough for about a week and it's not getting better.
Receptionist: I can fit you in tomorrow at 10:30. Does that work?
You: Yes, that's perfect. Thank you.
