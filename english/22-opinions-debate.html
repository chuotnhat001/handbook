Opinions & Debate

Y kien va tranh luan — cach trinh bay quan diem, dong y, phan doi, va thao luan lich su

Trinh bay y kien

Dua ra quan diem

I think / I believe... — Toi nghi / Toi tin rang...
In my opinion... — Theo y kien cua toi...
From my perspective... — Tu goc nhin cua toi...
The way I see it... — Theo cach toi nhin...
Personally, I feel that... — Ca nhan toi cam thay...
I'd argue that... — Toi cho rang... (manh hon)

Dong y

  • I agree. — Toi dong y.
  • That's a good point. — Do la mot y hay.
  • Exactly! / Absolutely! — Chinh xac!
  • I couldn't agree more. — Toi hoan toan dong y. (manh)
  • You have a point there. — Ban co ly o diem do.
  • I see what you mean. — Toi hieu y ban.

Phan doi lich su

  • I see your point, but... — Toi hieu y ban, nhung...
  • I'm not sure I agree. — Toi khong chac la dong y.
  • I respectfully disagree. — Toi xin phep khong dong y.
  • That's one way to look at it, but... — Do la mot cach nhin, nhung...
  • I take your point, however... — Toi ghi nhan y ban, tuy nhien...
  • I'd have to disagree on that. — Toi phai khong dong y o diem do.

Hoi y kien nguoi khac

  • What do you think about...? — Ban nghi gi ve...?
  • How do you feel about...? — Ban cam thay the nao ve...?
  • What's your take on...? — Quan diem cua ban ve...? (informal)
  • Do you agree that...? — Ban co dong y rang...?
  • Where do you stand on...? — Lap truong cua ban ve...?

Cau truc lap luan

Dua ra ly do:
The reason is... / This is because... / The main argument is...

Them vi du:
For example... / For instance... / Take [X] for example...

Nhuong buoc mot phan:
While I agree that [X], I still think [Y]...
It's true that [X], but on the other hand...

Ket luan:
So overall... / All things considered... / In conclusion...

So sanh voi tieng Viet

Giong: Ca hai ngon ngu deu co cac cap do lich su khi phan doi (tu nhe den manh).

Khac:

  • Tieng Anh khuyen khich phan doi truc tiep (direct disagreement) — duoc coi la binh thuong trong tranh luan. Tieng Viet thuong gian tiep hon de giu hoa khi.
  • Tieng Anh dung "I think" + menh de. Tieng Viet: "Toi nghi la..." — tuong tu nhung khong can "that".
  • Tieng Anh co hedging language (giam nhe): "I'm not sure", "I might be wrong, but..." — rat pho bien de lich su. Tieng Viet it dung kieu nay.
  • "I couldn't agree more" = dong y 100% (phu dinh kep = khang dinh manh). Tieng Viet khong co cau truc tuong duong.
  • Tieng Anh dung "however" / "on the other hand" de chuyen y. Tieng Viet: "tuy nhien" / "mat khac".

Dialogue mau

Tinh huong 1: Thao luan ve remote work

A: What's your take on remote work? Do you think it's better than working in an office?

B: Personally, I think remote work is great for productivity. You save time on commuting and have fewer distractions.

A: I see your point, but I'd argue that working in an office is better for collaboration. It's easier to brainstorm when you're in the same room.

B: That's true. But with tools like Zoom and Slack, you can still collaborate effectively. The key is having good communication habits.

A: You have a point there. I guess it depends on the type of work. For creative projects, I still prefer face-to-face.

B: I'd agree with that. Maybe a hybrid model is the best compromise — two or three days in the office, the rest remote.

A: Yeah, I think that's where most companies are heading. Best of both worlds.

Tinh huong 2: Tranh luan ve AI trong giao duc

A: I believe AI tools like ChatGPT should be allowed in schools. They help students learn faster.

B: I'm not sure I agree. If students rely on AI for everything, they won't develop critical thinking skills.

A: While I agree that over-reliance is a risk, I still think AI can be a powerful learning tool — like a calculator for writing. You still need to understand the fundamentals.

B: That's a fair point. But how do you prevent cheating? If a student submits AI-generated work, how would a teacher know?

A: Good question. I think the solution isn't to ban AI, but to redesign assessments. Focus on process, not just output.

B: I take your point. Maybe the real issue is how we assess learning, not the tools themselves.

A: Exactly. All things considered, I think we need to adapt rather than resist.

Loi thuong gap cua nguoi Viet

  • Sai: "I am agree with you." → Dung: "I agree with you." (agree la dong tu, khong can "am")
  • Sai: "According to me..." → Dung: "In my opinion..." (according to + nguon khac, khong dung cho chinh minh)
  • Sai: "I think it is not good." → Dung: "I don't think it's good." (phu dinh o "think", khong o menh de sau)
  • Sai: "I very agree." → Dung: "I strongly/completely agree." (khong dung "very" voi dong tu)
  • Sai: "In my opinion, I think..." → Dung: Chon mot: "In my opinion, [X]." HOAC "I think [X]." (khong dung ca hai — thua)
  • Sai: "I disagree you." → Dung: "I disagree with you." (can gioi tu "with")

Meo giao tiep

  • Hedging giup lich su hon: "I might be wrong, but..." / "I'm not entirely sure, but..." — giam nhe truoc khi phan doi.
  • Acknowledge truoc khi phan doi: "I see your point, but..." / "That's a valid argument, however..." — the hien ton trong.
  • Dung "I think" thay vi "You're wrong" — tap trung vao y kien cua minh, khong tan cong nguoi khac.
  • Hoi lai de hieu ro: "What do you mean by that?" / "Could you give an example?" — tranh hieu nham truoc khi phan doi.
  • Ket thuc bang diem chung: "I think we can both agree that..." — giup cuoc thao luan ket thuc tich cuc.

Bai tap

1. Sua loi: "I am agree with your opinion."

I agree with your opinion. (bo "am" — agree la dong tu)

2. Sua loi: "According to me, this is a bad idea."

In my opinion, this is a bad idea. ("according to" chi dung cho nguon khac)

3. Ban muon phan doi lich su. Chon cau tot nhat:
a) You're wrong.
b) That's stupid.
c) I see your point, but I'd have to disagree.

c) I see your point, but I'd have to disagree. (lich su va chuyen nghiep)

4. Sua loi: "I think it is not a good solution."

I don't think it's a good solution. (phu dinh o "think")

5. Dien vao cho trong: "I couldn't ___ more." (dong y hoan toan)

I couldn't agree more.

6. Ban muon hoi y kien dong nghiep ve du an moi. Hoi the nao? (2 cach)

What do you think about the new project? / What's your take on the new project?

7. Sua loi: "I very agree with this approach."

I strongly agree with this approach. (khong dung "very" voi dong tu)

8. Viet 2 cau: dong y mot phan nhung phan doi phan con lai.
Chu de: "Social media is bad for teenagers."

Vi du: While I agree that excessive social media use can be harmful, I don't think it's entirely bad — it also helps teenagers stay connected and learn new skills.

9. Sua loi: "In my opinion, I think we should cancel the meeting."

Chon mot: "In my opinion, we should cancel the meeting." HOAC "I think we should cancel the meeting." (khong dung ca hai)

10. Viet mot doan ngan (4-5 cau) trinh bay quan diem cua ban ve chu de: "Is it better to learn English online or in a classroom?"

Vi du: In my opinion, online learning is more effective for self-motivated learners. The main reason is flexibility — you can study at your own pace and choose materials that match your level. However, I'd argue that a classroom is better for beginners who need structure and immediate feedback from a teacher. All things considered, I think a combination of both is the ideal approach.
